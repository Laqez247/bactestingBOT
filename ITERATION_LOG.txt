================================================================================
SUMMARY OF WHAT HAS BEEN LEARNED
================================================================================

1. ZONE CONTEXT IS THE STRONGEST DISCRIMINATOR
   Every strategy tested shows the same pattern: trades that co-fire with
   an OB, FVG, or BB zone (Edge 2 trades) perform at 73–100% WR. Trades
   without zone context (pure CS1/CS2/CS4) perform at 44–65% WR. The zone
   provides structural evidence that the boundary has been accepted by the
   market. Without it, the signal is plausible but not validated.

2. STRUCTURE (MSS) IS THE SECOND STRONGEST DISCRIMINATOR
   Across CS1, CS2, and CS4: when the structure engine records MSS_BEARISH
   at the time of trade entry, WR is 77–88%. When structure is "unknown"
   (no MSS), WR is 44–65%. CS2's pure round-number fades at 44.7% WR are
   entirely explained by missing structure context.

3. CS1 LONG IS NOT A VIABLE SIGNAL ON XAUUSD
   LONG entries at range_low produced -0.0064R expectancy. Gold's volatility
   character means range_low breakdowns are more often genuine than fake-outs.
   The LONG direction was disabled in Iteration 3.

4. CS4 NEW YORK FAILS; LONDON WORKS
   Restricting CS4 to London session cut trades from 113 → 23 but improved
   WR from 44.2% → 65.2% and expectancy from -0.2753R → -0.031R. NY equity
   momentum prevents RSI-extreme bounces from working in gold.

5. WICK SIZE ALONE IS NOT A QUALITY FILTER
   The CS1 hard wick gate (Iteration 1) filtered 283 weak touches but
   expectancy DROPPED (+0.1263R → +0.0472R). Larger wicks on the surviving
   candles did not correlate with better outcomes because the underlying
   structural context was still absent. Wick size is necessary but not
   sufficient.

6. PRIOR SWEEP IS A STRONG QUALITY DISCRIMINATOR FOR CS1
   CS1 trades with a prior BSL sweep near range_high: 53 trades, ~74% WR,
   +0.35–0.49R expectancy. CS1 trades without prior sweep: 130 trades,
   64.6% WR, +0.13R expectancy. The sweep validates the boundary before
   CS1 fades it.

7. CS3 IS NOT FIRING
   CS3 produced only 5 trades across 25 months even after relaxing the
   body_trend threshold (0.65 → 0.80) and adding the ATR pre-filter. The
   strategy's micro-compression pattern is either too rare on XAUUSD 5min
   or the detection logic has additional filtering that prevents fires.
   No optimization iterations were run on CS3 yet.

8. MaxDD IS A VOLUME PROBLEM, NOT A WR PROBLEM
   CS1 with 130 trades at 35% loss rate produces a mathematically expected
   worst losing streak of ~7 consecutive losses = ~7R MaxDD. The only
   routes to ≤1.5R MaxDD contribution are: (a) reduce trade count to 10–15
   high-quality signals, or (b) achieve 85%+ WR. Adding gates to
   require sweep context at the boundary is the most data-supported path
   to reaching both goals simultaneously.

9. THE OVERALL PORTFOLIO WR MATH
   Edge 2 produces 85.4% WR on 41 trades. Adding 130 CS1 trades at 64.6%
   WR pulls the combined portfolio WR toward ~66%. The user target is 70%+
   portfolio WR. CS-strategy quality floors must effectively be 70%+ WR
   (not the spec minimums of 58–65%) to avoid diluting the Edge 2 premium.
