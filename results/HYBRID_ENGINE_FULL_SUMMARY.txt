SECTION 4: WHAT WAS NOT MERGED AND WHY
================================================================================

X  FIXED RR TARGET (detector.py — E2_RR setting)
   Reason: The new bot's dynamic TP engine (OB/FVG/BB/SR zone targets) is
   strictly better. Dynamic TP targets mean the risk/reward adapts to where
   real structural levels are, not an arbitrary multiplier. Fixed RR at 2.0
   forces exits at random prices. The dynamic engine produced 87.5% WR.
   Abandoning it would be a step backward.

X  24/5 SESSION TRADING (detector.py — no session filter)
   Reason: The new bot's London-only filter is one of its strongest edges.
   Asian session XAUUSD has low volatility and wide spreads. Adding NY or
   Asian sessions back would dilute the edge. 100% of all 41 final trades
   came from London session. WR = 82.9%. That is the data's verdict.

X  COMPRESSION ZONE AS ENTRY ZONE (detector.py — zone_type = compression)
   Reason: The new bot uses OB/FVG/BB/SR as retest zones after a sweep +
   breakout. These are structurally more meaningful than the compression zone
   boundary itself. OB zones in the final run: WR 87.5%, FVG: 100%, both far
   stronger than any compression-zone-only approach.

X  NO HTF FILTER (detector.py — no higher timeframe trend check)
   Reason: Gate 1 (HTF trend alignment) is a HARD GATE — it cannot be
   modified. 26 setups were rejected for HTF_NOT_BULLISH and 14 for
   HTF_NOT_BEARISH. These are correct rejections. Trading against the 1H
   trend would have produced losing trades. Your original bot traded these;
   the new bot correctly filters them.

X  NO MSS/BOS DISTINCTION (detector.py)
   Reason: Partially merged via MOD2, but the core MSS requirement is kept
   as default. MSS trades in the final run: 83-88% WR. BOS trades via MOD2:
   66.7% WR. The distinction matters and the data confirms it.

X  PHASE 2 OVERRIDE THRESHOLDS (legacy new-bot code — too strict)
   Reason: The Phase 2 framework (4 overrides: MOMENTUM, CONFLUENCE, SWEEP_
   MAGNITUDE, CONTEXT) used thresholds that were too high for real data.
   Example: MOMENTUM_OVERRIDE required body >= 1.5x ATR — but 0 out of 26
   MSS_REQUIRED rejections had body >= 1.5x ATR. The overrides fired exactly
   1 time in the entire 2-year dataset, and that 1 trade lost.
   Solution: Replaced with the 5-modification layer at lower, data-calibrated
   thresholds, guarded by the regime detector instead of raw score thresholds.

SECTION 5: NEW COMPONENT — REGIME DETECTOR (regime_detector.py)
================================================================================

This is entirely new — it has no equivalent in your original files.

What it does:
  - Runs at every setup attempt (bar-by-bar, no lookahead)
  - Analyzes the last 120 bars (5 days) of 1H data
  - Detects swing highs and lows (pivot left=2, right=2 on 1H)
  - Counts consecutive HH+HL sequences (bullish) or LH+LL sequences (bearish)
  - Computes ATR ratio: current ATR / 30-bar ATR mean

Classifications:
  TRENDING_STRONG    : >= 5 qualifying swing pairs + no violent counter-trend
                       1H candles + ATR within normal range (0.7–2.5x mean)
                       Allowed modifications: MOD1, MOD2, MOD3, MOD4, MOD5

  TRENDING_MODERATE  : >= 3 qualifying swing pairs + ATR normal
                       Allowed modifications: MOD1, MOD3, MOD4, MOD5
                       (NOT MOD2 — BOS acceptance requires strong trend only)

  RANGING            : < 3 qualifying swing pairs
                       Allowed modifications: MOD3 only (confluence-stale-retest)

  HIGH_VOLATILITY    : ATR > 2.5x its 30-bar mean
                       Allowed modifications: NONE — all modifications blocked

  LOW_STRUCTURE      : Absolute ATR < 0.70
                       Allowed modifications: NONE

Why this is better than a raw score threshold:
  The old Phase 2 framework used a single "compensation score" (0-100 points
  from various factors). A high score could come from many different
  combinations of factors, some of which don't actually justify a soft gate
  override. The regime detector asks a single, concrete structural question:
  "Is the macro market trending right now, and how strongly?" That is a far
  more meaningful guard than an abstract score.
